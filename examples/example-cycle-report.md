# Example Cycle Report

This example demonstrates a completed cycle on a neutral piece of material. It shows both a PASS outcome at most stations and a HOLD at one station, resulting in an overall HOLD outcome with a clear reinjection note.

**Date:** 2024-03-15  
**Operator:** Editorial team member  
**Material ID:** MATERIAL-EXAMPLE-001  
**Material type:** Draft essay  
**Cycle version:** v0.1

---

## 1. Material status at start of cycle

**Status:** Draft — first review pass pending  
**Prior cycles:** None  
**Notes:** The essay was submitted by a team member for first-cycle evaluation. It has not been shared externally. No prior cycle reports exist.

---

## 2. Local context

This cycle is the first structured pass on this draft essay. The operator is running the cycle independently using the run-cycle prompt. No special constraints apply. The goal of this cycle is to establish a baseline status and identify whether the essay is ready for structured review.

---

## 3. Production readiness

**Readiness assessment:** PARTIAL  
**Gate decision:** PASS  
**Rationale:** The essay has a clear topic and an identifiable argument. It is incomplete in one section (section 3 lacks a conclusion), but it is sufficiently developed to proceed with review. The incomplete section is noted for follow-up.

---

## 4. Fragment body

**Fragment identified:** YES  
**Fragment description:** The full draft essay, approximately 1,200 words, structured in four sections. Section 3 is incomplete.  
**Gate decision:** PASS

---

## 5. Review

**Review summary:** The essay was reviewed for coherence, completeness, internal consistency and fitness for its stated purpose (an argumentative essay suitable for a research-adjacent publication context).

**Issues found:**

1. Section 3 lacks a concluding paragraph. The argument breaks off mid-development.
2. The transition between sections 2 and 3 is abrupt. A bridging sentence would improve flow.
3. The essay's claim in paragraph 4 is asserted without supporting evidence. A reference or qualifier is needed.

**Gate decision:** HOLD  
**Rationale:** The essay has clear strengths and a viable argument. However, the incomplete section and unsubstantiated claim constitute substantive issues that must be addressed before the essay can pass the review station. The hold is not a rejection. It is a return condition.

---

## 6. Blueprint / reusable pattern test

**Pattern found:** YES  
**Pattern description:** The essay uses a structuring approach in sections 1 and 2 that moves from a general framing question to a specific operational constraint. This move — general framing → specific constraint — is a pattern that may be reusable in other argumentative essays and in research note structuring.  
**Gate decision:** EXTRACT_ONLY

**Overall outcome:** HOLD

**What was read:**  
A 1,200-word draft essay with four sections. Section 3 is incomplete. The essay has a clear argument but contains substantive issues at the review station.

**What status the material has now:**  
HOLD at Station 5 (Review). The essay remains in draft status.

**Gates passed:**  
- Station 1: Read status — PASS  
- Station 2: Read local context — PASS

**Gates held:**

**What can happen next:**  
The author may address the three issues identified in the review (complete section 3, improve the section 2–3 transition, add support for the claim in paragraph 4) and request a new cycle.

**What must not happen next:**  
- The essay must not be submitted for publication or shared externally while on hold.
- The hold must not be overridden without a new cycle that addresses the identified issues.

**Artefacts produced:**

## Notes and observations

The reinjection note captures a genuine structural pattern from sections 1 and 2 of the essay. This extraction is independent of the hold status. The pattern is available for reuse regardless of whether the essay eventually proceeds or stops.

The three hold conditions are specific and addressable. This is a well-formed HOLD: clear rationale, clear conditions, clear path to a new cycle.
