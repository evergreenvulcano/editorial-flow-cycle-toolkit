# Concept

## What this is

The Editorial Flow Cycle Toolkit is a structured framework for moving material through a gated editorial cycle.

It is not a content pipeline. It is not a publishing system. It is a flow system.

The distinction matters:

- A **content pipeline** assumes that material should move forward toward publication.
- A **flow system** assumes that material should move through structured evaluation, and that the evaluation may result in any valid outcome — including stopping.

## Core insight

Most editorial and creative teams need a flow system before they need a publishing system.

Publishing pipelines are optimized for throughput: move material from idea to distribution as efficiently as possible. That model works for high-volume content production.

It fails for sensitive, experimental, literary, strategic or early-stage material where:

- the correct next step may be to hold
- the material may produce a method without becoming content
- public readiness and editorial readiness are different questions
- stopping is sometimes the best outcome

## What a cycle does

A cycle is a single structured pass through a piece of material.

It reads the material's current status, moves it through a series of stations, checks gates at each critical transition point, and produces a cycle report.

The cycle does not automatically promote material. Promotion requires a human gate decision.

## What a gate is

A gate is a local judgment point.

A gate is not an automatic approval. It is a named checkpoint at which a person (or an AI assistant operating under explicit instructions) evaluates whether a specific condition has been met.

Gates produce decisions. Decisions are recorded. Decisions determine whether material may continue, hold, return, or stop.

See [`gates.md`](gates.md) for the full gate model.

## What a cycle report is

A cycle report is the default deliverable of any completed cycle.

It records:

- what was read
- what status was assigned at each station
- which gate decisions were made
- what the next possible action is
- what must not happen next
- any learning that can be reused

The cycle report is not a publication. It is not a draft. It is an operational record.

## What reinjection means

Reinjection is the act of taking learning from one cycle and making it available for future cycles or other materials.

A reinjection note captures the pattern, method or insight without duplicating the source material.

The source material is not promoted by being referenced in a reinjection note.

## Principle summary

1. Flow is allowed.
2. Promotion is gated.
3. Stopping is a valid output.
4. Review is a station, not a delay.
5. A material can produce method without becoming content.
6. A public candidate is not a publication.
7. A repo surface is not automatically a product.
8. Provenance matters, but source material should not be duplicated unless explicitly authorized.
9. The cycle creates reports by default, artefacts only when gates pass.
10. The system must be usable by humans, Copilot and other assistants without hidden context.
